## 1. Operational Definition
This bridge defines the gates, failure signals, and verification checks required to transition a learner from early cognitive foundation building (Ontogeny) into active human-AI co-agency (Praxis).

## 2. Theoretical Foundation
Draws from Piaget's transition from concrete operations to formal operations, requiring active cognitive struggle (desirable difficulty) to construct mature mental schemas.

## 3. Cartesian Axis Mapping
- **Ontogeny Coordinate ($X$):** 3.0 (Concrete-Abstract transition boundary)
- **Praxis Coordinate ($Y$):** 2.5 (Attempted friction initiation)
- **Autonomy Coordinate ($Z$):** 2.0 (Guided selection of challenges)

## 4. Core Mechanics / Dynamics
1. The learner's progress along the Ontogeny scale is tracked via their mastery of concrete conceptual nodes.
2. Once the threshold is met, the system initiates the **Friction Escalation Loop**, prompting the user with contradictions rather than simple validations.
3. The bridge is successfully crossed when the learner stops accepting default AI claims and begins actively generating counter-claims.

## 6. Transition Gates / Failure Signals
### Entry Gates
* **Conceptual Competence:** Learner must successfully validate understanding of concrete foundation nodes (Ontogeny index $\ge$ 2.5).
* **Questioning Prompt:** Active generation of at least two independent queries regarding the target concepts.

### Exit Gates
* **Counter-Argumentation:** User initiates a debate or challenges a machine premise rather than accepting it (Praxis index $\ge$ 4.0).
* **Reasoning Path Log:** Completion of at least one multi-step reasoning trail tracking custom assumptions.

### Failure Signals
* **Compliance Atrophy:** The learner accepts three consecutive machine-generated claims without querying or asking for alternatives.
* **Copy-Paste Regression:** Rapid copy-pasting of AI outputs into work fields without intermediate edits or revisions.
* **Time-to-Ingestion Drop:** Reading/processing times drop below 5 seconds per major concept block, indicating passive skimming.

## 7. Cognitive Risk Mitigations
- Mitigates: [[cognitive_offloading]]
- Mechanism of defense: Refuses to provide direct summaries or answers once the user hits the bridge threshold, forcing them to formulate their own outlines first.

## 8. Anti-Patterns
- **Premature Escalation:** Forcing high-friction Socratic sparring before the learner has established basic conceptual vocabulary (Ontogeny < 2.5).

## 9. Observable Markers
- System logs indicating an increase in user edit-distance on workspace text inputs.
- Elevated rates of Socratic questions selected/typed during interactive sessions.
