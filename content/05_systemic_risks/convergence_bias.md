## 1. Operational Definition
Convergence Bias is the loss of intellectual diversity and eccentric arguments as learners align their conceptual definitions and solutions to match the standard consensus generated by AI models.

## 2. Theoretical Foundation
Draws from statistical learning theory, network effects, and cognitive conformity (social proof applied to machine consensus).

## 3. Cartesian Axis Mapping
- **Ontogeny Coordinate ($X$):** 5.0
- **Praxis Coordinate ($Y$):** 1.0 (Low divergent thinking)
- **Autonomy Coordinate ($Z$):** 4.0

## 4. Core Mechanics / Dynamics
1. The user seeks brainstorming ideas from the AI.
2. The AI outputs the most probable statistical associations (commonplace ideas).
3. The user selects these suggestions because they feel safe and familiar.
4. Unique, high-friction, or radical ideas are discarded in favor of generic, homogenized structures.
