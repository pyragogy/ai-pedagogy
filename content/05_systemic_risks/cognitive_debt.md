## 1. Operational Definition
Cognitive Debt is the long-term structural decay of a learner's capacity for deep focus, critical reasoning, and original synthesis, resulting from chronic over-reliance on frictionless digital automation.

## 2. Theoretical Foundation
Analogy of technical debt in software engineering applied to cognitive systems: shortcutting cognitive processes yields fast output today at the cost of diminished capacity tomorrow.

## 3. Cartesian Axis Mapping
- **Ontogeny Coordinate ($X$):** 6.0 (Affects mature learners)
- **Praxis Coordinate ($Y$):** 0.0 (Zero active friction)
- **Autonomy Coordinate ($Z$):** 3.0

## 4. Core Mechanics / Dynamics
1. The learner uses AI to bypass all cognitive obstacles over months.
2. Synaptic connections associated with critical verification and deep focus atrophy.
3. The learner's tolerance for intellectual discomfort (confusion, research dead-ends) drops.
4. Eventually, the learner is completely unable to solve non-trivial problems independently.

## 8. Anti-Patterns
- **The Shortcut Trap:** Relying on tools that "write for you" under the guise of increasing productivity, without realizing it is eroding your raw ability to write.

## 9. Observable Markers
- Clear drop in the length and depth of self-written notes over a multi-month period.
- Frequent symptoms of cognitive fatigue or frustration when trying to read papers without summaries.
